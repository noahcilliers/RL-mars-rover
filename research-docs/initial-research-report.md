# Deep Research on Reinforcement Learning for Mars Surface Infrastructure Preparation

## Key findings

The strongest conclusion from the current landscape is that there is **no mature open-source environment that already combines** all of the pieces your target agent needs in one place: **Mars terrain**, **robot mobility/manipulation**, **resource extraction and accounting**, **site selection**, **construction sequencing**, and **habitability-prep objectives**. Instead, the ecosystem breaks into three useful layers: **planetary robotics simulators** for movement and sensing, **operations/scheduling environments** for allocation and sequencing, and **game-like open-ended worlds** for long-horizon resource and build planning. That means the practical path is not “find the one perfect Mars PPO environment,” but **compose a stack** and unify it with your own Mars mission state model. This is an inference from the surveyed environment families below. citeturn22view0turn19view1turn20view0turn25view0turn10view5turn10view9

If you want the **closest immediate fit for PPO on a Mars-like robotics problem**, the best open-source starting points are **MarsExplorer** for lightweight exploration/site-recon baselines, **Space Robotics Bench** for Isaac-Sim-based extraterrestrial robotics with Gymnasium and ROS 2 integration, and **RLRoverLab** for rover-centric navigation/manipulation training on Isaac Sim / Isaac Lab. These are the most direct bridges between PPO-friendly tooling and planetary surface autonomy. citeturn22view0turn19view1turn20view0turn29view5

If your real target is not only mobility but **autonomous base preparation**—choosing where to build, assigning robots, scheduling delivery and assembly, and balancing multi-objective constraints—then the best analogues are currently **JobShopLab**, **TA-RWARE**, **HeteroMRTA**, and the recent **MAPPO-based collaborative task-and-path planning** work. These are not Mars-native, but they are structurally closer to habitat-prep than most rover papers. citeturn25view0turn25view1turn25view2turn15view11

For **game and sim analogues**, the closest transfer targets are **Factorio Learning Environment** for automation and resource throughput, **Minecraft-family environments** such as **MineDojo**, **MineRL**, **Project Malmo**, and **Craftium** for open-ended building and survival, plus **Crafter**, **microRTS**, and **Neural MMO** for reduced-cost studies of long-horizon planning, economies, and multi-agent coordination. These are not Mars-specific, but they are highly relevant for curriculum design and ablation studies. citeturn10view5turn10view0turn10view3turn12view3turn10view9turn10view8turn10view7turn12view2turn32view5

The best analogues are therefore environments that make an agent decide **what to do next, with which resource, in what order, under uncertainty and bottlenecks**. citeturn25view0turn25view1turn25view2turn15view11

## Recommended stack for a first Mars habitability-prep agent

If I were choosing a stack for a serious PPO-driven Mars infrastructure project today, I would split the problem into **three coupled training layers** rather than one monolithic environment. That recommendation is an inference from the capabilities and gaps in the surveyed tools. citeturn22view0turn19view1turn25view0turn10view5

For the **surface robotics layer**, I would use **Space Robotics Bench** or **RLRoverLab** on Isaac Sim / Isaac Lab. SRB is the better choice if you want a more general extraterrestrial benchmark architecture with Gymnasium, ROS 2, procedural generation, domain randomization, and large-scale simulation. RLRoverLab is the better choice if you want a rover-oriented suite you can extend quickly. citeturn19view1turn20view0

For the **mission-operations layer**, I would use **JobShopLab** for sequencing, bottlenecks, machine/vehicle breakdowns, and multi-objective optimization, then fold in **HeteroMRTA** or the recent **MAPPO task/path planning** work for multi-robot assignment. This is the layer that teaches the agent when to excavate, when to move stockpiles, when to place power, and how to schedule parallel construction crews. citeturn25view0turn25view2turn15view11

For the **open-ended curriculum layer**, I would use **Factorio Learning Environment** and **Craftium**. FLE is the cleanest benchmark for “grow an infrastructure graph under resource constraints.” Craftium is the best open-source sandbox for turning that abstract competency into actual 3D construction tasks. citeturn10view5turn10view9

A sensible development path would look like this. Start with **MarsExplorer** to validate PPO reward design for reconnaissance and site selection. Move to **SRB/RLRoverLab** for realistic perception and mobility. Add a separate **scheduler policy** trained in **JobShopLab** and multi-robot allocation policies from **HeteroMRTA** or **MAPPO-style task/path planning**. Finally, use **Factorio/Craftium** as curriculum environments for long-horizon infrastructure dependencies before integrating everything into a mission-level Mars sim. That staged approach matches how NASA’s operational autonomy itself is decomposed across navigation, science targeting, and planning. citeturn22view0turn19view1turn20view0turn25view0turn25view2turn15view11turn27search7turn27search9turn27search10

The clearest short list is therefore:

**Best lightweight PPO baseline:** **MarsExplorer.** citeturn22view0

**Best high-fidelity open-source extraterrestrial robotics base:** **Space Robotics Bench.** citeturn19view1

**Best rover-focused Isaac starting point:** **RLRoverLab.** citeturn20view0

**Best sequencing and resource-management analogue:** **JobShopLab.** citeturn25view0

**Best infrastructure-growth analogue:** **Factorio Learning Environment.** citeturn10view5turn33view0

**Best customizable open-source 3D colony-construction sandbox:** **Craftium.** citeturn10view9turn14view8

If your goal is to end up with a publishable system that feels genuinely “Mars habitat prep” rather than “rover navigation plus unrelated scheduling,” the strongest research contribution would probably be to **build the missing integrated benchmark** on top of **Isaac + Gymnasium + a mission-operations layer**, then benchmark PPO, hierarchical PPO, and MAPPO-style coordinator policies against classical planners and heuristics. The surveyed landscape suggests that this benchmark does not currently exist in open-source form, and creating it would itself be a meaningful contribution. citeturn19view1turn25view0turn15view11turn10view5turn22view0
